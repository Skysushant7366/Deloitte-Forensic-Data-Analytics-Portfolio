3. Project 1: Daikibo Machine Telemetry Analysis (Tableau)
Objective: Perform a large-scale analysis of operational telemetry across four global manufacturing hubs (Tokyo, Osaka, Berlin, and Shenzhen) to identify critical reliability issues and machine-driven downtime
.
Technical Execution & Methodology:
Schema Ingestion: Handled the daikibo-telemetry-data.json.zip file, ensuring all multi-level hierarchies and data hierarchies were preserved
.
Calculated Measure Engineering: Developed a custom "Unhealthy" status measure, assigning a weight of 10 to every unhealthy status message to represent 10 minutes of potential downtime
.
Visualization Architecture: Developed bar charts for "Down Time per Factory" and "Down Time per Device Type" to isolate outliers
.
Quantitative Insights:
Osaka (Seiko) was identified as the factory with the highest failure rate, followed by Shenzhen, while Berlin showed minimal downtime
.
LaserWelders and LaserCutters were identified as the primary device types driving operational downtime globally
.

4. Project 2: Forensic Gender Pay Equality Audit (Excel)
Objective: Conducted a forensic investigation to audit gender-based salary inequality across global operations following internal complaints
.
Forensic Methodology:
Nested Logic Classification: Implemented a tiered "Equality class" classification in Excel using nested IF statements to interpret Equality Scores ranging from -100 to +100
.
Data Classification Logic:
Fair: Equality Score between -10 and +10
.
Unfair: Score between -20 and -10 OR +10 and +20
.
Highly Discriminative: Score < -20 OR > +20
.
Impact: Provided a prioritized roadmap for intervention by pinpointing job roles and factory locations with the most severe pay gaps
.
